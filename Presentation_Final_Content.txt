AAES FINAL PRESENTATION CONTENT (COMPREHENSIVE - ALL FEATURES INCLUDED)

1. ABSTRACT
• Core Purpose: AAES is an end-to-end, AI-driven educational platform designed to automate assignment evaluation, streamline institutional governance, and facilitate continuous student mentorship.
• Technologies Employed: Optical Character Recognition (OCR), Google Gemini LLM for semantic grading of theory/essays, and highly secure Sandboxed Python Execution for programming assignments.
• Stakeholder Reach: Features distinct, fully-featured modules for 6 unique roles: Students, Teaching Staff, Faculty Advisors (Mentors), Heads of Department (HOD), Principal, and System Administrators.

2. PROBLEM STATEMENTS
• Manual Grading Overload: Teaching staff spend hours manually grading subjective answers or debugging student code, leading to burnout.
• Subjective Inconsistencies: Human evaluators often display unintentional bias or fatigue-driven errors during grading.
• Siloed Mentorship Data: Faculty Mentors lack a unified, cross-subject view of their mentees' academic health and attendance.
• Reactive Governance: Institutional leaders (HOD/Principal) only identify struggling students or poor faculty engagement at the end of the semester.
• Fragmented Communication: Traditional Class Committee Meetings (CCM) often fail to track student grievances to resolution.

3. LITERATURE SURVEY
• Traditional Learning Management Systems (LMS): Existing systems (like Moodle/Canvas) only support basic MCQ or keyword-matching automation, failing on complex theory or handwritten logic.
• Evaluation Gap: Current programming graders do not efficiently integrate with subjective theory evaluators in the same ecosystem.
• AI Potential: The advent of robust LLMs and advanced OCR provides a novel opportunity to semantically evaluate long-form, subjective student submissions with high accuracy.

4. EXISTING SYSTEM
• Submission Workflow: Students submit physical paper copies or email scattered digital files (PDFs, Word docs, raw code files).
• Evaluation Mechanism: Teaching staff print out code or manually read essays, guessing code logic without executing it securely.
• Mentorship & CCM: Counseling logs, student grievances, and mentorship tracking are maintained in easily misplaced physical registers.
• Reporting: Academic health and risk analysis are calculated manually in Excel, often weeks after exams conclude.

5. EXISTING SYSTEM DISADVANTAGES
• Slow Feedback Loop: Turnaround time of days or weeks before students know their mistakes.
• High Error Margins: Prone to fatigue-based grading mistakes and lack of standardized rubrics.
• Opaque Evaluation: Students rarely receive detailed, line-by-line reasoning for deducted marks.
• Administrative Friction: Managing physical records for thousands of students creates a nightmare for audits and accreditation (like NAAC/NBA).

6. PROPOSED SYSTEM (AAES)
• Intelligent, Hybrid Grading: Combines AI-driven semantic comparison against teacher-provided model answers with Automated Unit Testing for executable code.
• Centralized Academic Hub: A single platform integrating Assignments, Mentorship, Attendance, CCM, and High-Level Governance Analytics.
• Real-Time Risk Intelligence: Dynamic dashboards instantly flag students with low attendance or failing grades.
• Secure Cloud Execution: Isolated environments for safe, hacker-proof student code execution and instantaneous testing.

7. PROPOSED SYSTEM ADVANTAGES
• Near-Instant Results: Highly accurate grading results and personalized feedback are generated in mere seconds.
• Objective & Standardized Marking: Eliminates human bias, ensuring fair grading for every single student according to strict rubrics.
• Proactive Intervention: Automated "Risk Intelligence" allows mentors to intervene with struggling students early in the semester.
• Massive Efficiency Gains: Reduces teacher evaluation time by over 90%, freeing them up for active research and personalized teaching.

8. COMPREHENSIVE MODULE & FEATURE DESCRIPTIONS
A. STUDENT MODULE
• Multi-Format Submissions: Upload Handwritten documents, Word Docs, PDFs, PPTs, or write/execute raw Code directly in the browser.
• Instant AI Feedback: View detailed AI analysis explaining exactly why marks were awarded or deducted per question.
• Re-evaluation Workflows: Students can formally request structured resubmissions if they perform poorly.
• Unified View: Track upcoming deadlines, view overall attendance percentages, and monitor subject-wise progression.

B. STAFF (TEACHER) MODULE
• Advanced Assignment Creation: Configure customized assignments (Theory, Code, PPT) with test cases, grading rubrics, allowed formats, and late-submission penalties.
• Grading Overrides: Staff retain ultimate authority to review AI scores, edit marks manually, add private feedback, and "Publish" final marks to the Gradebook.
• Mark Locking & Unlocking: Staff can "Lock" a gradebook to finalize it. Any subsequent changes require formal "Unlock Requests" approved by the HOD.
• Class Analytics: Dashboard showing grade distributions, top/bottom performers, and real-time class attendance data.

C. ADVISOR (MENTOR) MODULE
• Assigned Mentee Tracking: Dedicated portal tracking an assigned group of students across ALL their enrolled subjects, not just the mentor's own class.
• Counseling & Interaction Logs: Secure digital logging of 1-on-1 counseling sessions, behavioral notes, and academic guidance.
• Early Warnings: Mentors are instantly alerted if their mentee drops below required attendance or fails multiple subjects.

D. CLASS COMMITTEE MEETING (CCM) PORTAL
• Digital Minutes: Transition from paper registers to a digital CCM portal.
• Attendee & Feedback Tracking: Log participating students, record specific subject grievances or feedback.
• Actionable Follow-Ups: Map feedback to actionable items, ensuring student concerns are addressed systematically.

E. HEAD OF DEPARTMENT (HOD) MODULE
• Departmental Audits: Top-down view of all active subjects within their department.
• Regional Performance: Track and compare faculty grading trends, ensuring departmental alignment.
• Administrative Approvals: Review and approve staff requests to unlock finalized gradebooks for corrections.

F. PRINCIPAL (INSTITUTIONAL) MODULE
• Ultimate Institutional Dashboard: Complete oversight of the entire college's academic operation.
• Risk Intelligence Heatmaps: Automatically identifies the most "At-Risk" students across the institution for targeted intervention.
• Exportable Reporting: Download comprehensive Principal Risk Reports as cleanly formatted PDFs for immediate accreditation/board review.

G. ADMINISTRATION MODULE
• Bulk Onboarding: One-click CSV imports to create hundreds of user accounts, subjects, and student enrollments instantly.
• System Lifecycle Management: Features like "Semester Transitions" to automatically archive old data, promote students to the next academic year, and reset roles securely.

9. SPECIFIC FEATURE ENHANCEMENTS AND OPTIONS INCLUDED
• Automatic Plagiarism Detection: Checks submissions for AI-to-AI similarities and flags abnormally high matches.
• Configurable Deadlines & Rules: Granular options to allow/disable submissions globally, extend specific deadlines, and apply percentage-based (-10%, -20%) late penalties automatically.
• "Safe Mode" Code Execution: Students cannot crash the main server even if they write infinite loops or malicious code.

10. HARDWARE AND SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS
• Software Stack (Full MERN + Python): React (Frontend), Node.js & Express (Backend APIs), MongoDB (Database), Python & FastAPI (Evaluation Engine), Google Tesseract (OCR), and Local/Cloud LLM Integrations.
• Server Hardware: Minimum 2.5GHz Quad-core Processor, 8GB-16GB RAM, 10GB-50GB SSD Storage.
• Client Requirements: Standard modern Web Browser (Chrome, Edge, Safari) with active internet.

11. DETAILED WORKFLOW DIAGRAM
1. File Upload -> Student securely submits a document (Image/PDF/Docs for Theory) or Script (Python).
2. Routing Engine -> Identifies submission type and routes to the appropriate microservice.
3. Processing (Theory Action): Image extraction -> OCR to readable text -> AI semantic comparison against Staff Model Answer -> Intelligent Score & Feedback generation.
4. Processing (Code Action): Student Code -> Pushed to Secure Sandbox Execution -> Run against predefined Unit Tests -> Capture stdout/stderr/errors -> Calculate final Score.
5. Finalization: Results securely aggregated in MongoDB -> Real-time UI updates on Staff & Student Dashboards -> Notifications fired.

12. SCREENSHOT & UI HIGHLIGHTS
• Premium Aesthetics: High-fidelity, responsive dashboards optimized for all 6 roles, utilizing modern UI/UX framing.
• Interactive Analytics: Intelligent Risk Maps, responsive pie charts for attendance, and grade distribution line graphs.
• Deep-Dive Evaluation UI: Detailed, split-screen displays comparing student answers alongside AI-generated feedback and staff-adjusted scores.
• Digital Governance: Showcasing the sleek CCM records and comprehensive Advisor counseling interaction logs.

13. CONCLUSION & IMPACT
• Transformative Tech: AAES modernizes higher education by transforming passive grading data into "Actionable Intelligence" for faculty and administration.
• Administrative Relief: Significantly slashes administrative friction and grading fatigue.
• Scalability: Provides a fully transparent, highly scalable, and instantaneous evaluation ecosystem perfectly suited for the future of digital-first academic institutions.
